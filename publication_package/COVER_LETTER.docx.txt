SIGNIFICANCE OF THE WORK
------------------------
Earthquake prediction remains one of the most challenging problems in geophysics. Our study addresses this challenge by developing and comparing deep learning models for detecting earthquake precursors from geomagnetic data. The key contributions include:

1. A comprehensive comparative analysis of VGG16 and EfficientNet-B0 architectures for multi-task earthquake precursor classification
2. Development of a novel dataset comprising 1,972 spectrograms from 256 earthquake events in Indonesia
3. Achievement of 98.68% magnitude classification accuracy (VGG16) with physically interpretable features
4. Rigorous cross-validation: LOEO (97.53%) and LOSO (97.57%) demonstrating excellent generalization
5. Demonstration that EfficientNet-B0 offers 26Ã— smaller model size suitable for operational deployment
6. Grad-CAM analysis confirming models focus on scientifically meaningful ULF frequency patterns
7. Comprehensive failure analysis with honest disclosure of limitations

NOVELTY AND RELEVANCE
--------------------
This work is novel in several aspects:
- First comprehensive comparison of modern CNN architectures for geomagnetic precursor detection
- Multi-task learning framework for simultaneous magnitude and azimuth prediction
- Rigorous validation preventing data leakage through event-level splitting (LOEO) and station-level splitting (LOSO)
- Explainability analysis linking model attention to established geophysical theory
- Transparent reporting of limitations including rare class statistical power and normal class selection criteria
